Our Why: We're Doing It For The Little Guy

Mr. Salvador-Casillas Alonso was born in Coalcoman, Mexico. At 18, he immigrated to the United States in the 1970’s where he landed a job at the Matsui Nursery in Salinas, California. After dedicating 20+ years of his life to the company, he was not making much more than the minimum wage. With a growing family and 10 hour work days, he was failing to be the father, he originally set out to be.


In 2003, he took a gamble and started a septic service company by driving the family in their minivan to Texarkana, AR from California and purchasing his first piece of equipment. With the family savings on the line, minimal experience in the sector, and English as a second language – it took guts.


Today, Mr. Alonso has finished paying his house off, put three children through college – one of whom will be attending medical school in the coming year. When asked what drove him to start a septic company he states, “I did it to get the luxury of driving my kids to school in the mornings. Before the money, and before everything else, I simply wanted to spend time with my family and see them grow.”


After helping set up the company at 13, Mr. Alonso’s son, Sal Casillas Jr., ventured out to the unknown reaching the highest levels of education while becoming involved with prominent world-renowned companies along the way in San Francisco, but there was something missing. An itch he couldn't shake off. Back in Salinas, his old man was still working as hard as he had at the start, and now he was doing it by himself.

PumperPal is for small business owners who have tradionally been ignored. For the sons and daughters who grind it out with their folks, asking themselves what is an easier way to do this. I started PumperPal to make my father's job easier and now I have the opportunity to extend that help to others. Everyone deserves dignity and respect for their work.
